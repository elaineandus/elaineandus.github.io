In this study, we aimed to compare the before and after effects of implementing a kiosk machine system in a fast food restaurant, specifically at the Jollibee branch in SM City Manila. Fast food restaurants like Jollibee had a large customer base due to their popularity and affordability, resulting in long queues and customer dissatisfaction. To address this problem, a kiosk machine system was introduced. These self-ordering kiosks provided a convenient and contact-free ordering experience, reducing the waiting time at the counter and improving customer service. To analyze the impact of the kiosk system, we utilized a discrete-event simulation approach using Simio Simulation Software. By simulating the queuing system before and after the implementation of the kiosk machines, we could determine the effectiveness of this solution. The simulation software allowed us to specify data points and evaluate the number of clients served within a given time unit. Additionally, EasyFit software was used to identify the distribution of service length and arrival time at the restaurant. By combining these tools, we developed queue models for the Jollibee fast food restaurant and compared their performance. This study contributed to the field of simulation modeling and provided insights into optimizing queuing systems in the fast food industry. By analyzing the impact of implementing a kiosk machine system in a Jollibee fast food restaurant, we shed light on the effectiveness of this solution and its potential benefits for customer satisfaction and service efficiency. These findings informed future decision-making processes for fast food establishments seeking to improve their queuing systems and enhance overall customer experience.
